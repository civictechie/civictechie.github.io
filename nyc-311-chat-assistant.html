1. Project Brief

Project Name: NYC 311 AI Chat Assistant

Objective: Develop an intelligent conversational assistant to supplement NYC's 311 service by enabling residents to report non-emergency incidents, request city services, and access information through natural language conversation.

  • Collect and process incident reports (potholes, noise complaints, sanitation issues, etc.)
  • Provide information about city services and programs
  • Track existing 311 requests

5. Bot Responses

Response Style Framework

A. Empathetic Tone

Used when: User expresses distress, frustration, or urgent need

Empathetic Response Example 
User: "There's no heat in my apartment and it's freezing"

Bot: "I understand how uncomfortable and concerning that must be, 
especially in this cold weather. Let me help you report this right away 
and connect you with emergency resources. 

This is a serious issue - buildings must maintain heat between October 
and May. I can:
1. File an emergency heat complaint with HPD
2. Provide the tenant helpline number (212-HEAT)
3. Share information about emergency shelters if needed

Which would be most helpful right now?"

B. Informational Tone

Used when: User seeks factual information or guidance

Informational Response Example 
User: "When is my recycling picked up?"

Bot: "I can help you find your collection schedule! 

To look this up, I'll need your address or borough. Metal, glass, 
plastic, and beverage cartons are collected on the same day as your 
trash, just in separate bins.

What's your address or which borough are you in?"

C. Actionable/Directive Tone

Used when: Guiding users through processes or next steps

Actionable Response Example 
User: "I want to report a pothole"

Bot: "I'll help you report that pothole right now. To create an 
effective service request, I need a few details:

üìç **Location** - What's the exact address or nearest intersection?
üìè **Size** - How large is it? (small/medium/large)
üì∏ **Photo** - Can you upload a picture? (optional but helpful)

Let's start with the location - where is this pothole?"

Response Principles

  1. Clarity First: Use simple language, avoid jargon
  2. Progressive Disclosure: Don't overwhelm with information
  3. Visual Structure: Use emojis, line breaks, numbered lists
  4. Confirmation: Always summarize what was understood/completed
  5. Next Steps: Always provide clear path forward
  6. Multilingual Sensitivity: Offer language options proactively
